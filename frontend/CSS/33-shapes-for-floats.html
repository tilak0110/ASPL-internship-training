Chapter 33: Shapes for Floats

This text wraps around a circular image thanks to the `shape-outside: circle()` property. It allows us to float elements in a way that feels more natural and visually engaging. Notice how the text follows the circular outline instead of wrapping around a square box. This is particularly useful in magazine-style layouts and custom blog designs.

We’ve also applied `shape-margin` to ensure that the text doesn’t sit directly on the edge of the image. That gives it breathing space and improves readability. These techniques can only be applied to floated elements, and the image must be visible for shape detection to work.
